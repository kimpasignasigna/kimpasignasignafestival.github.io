Sinulog Festival
DESCRIPTION

The Sinulog Festival is a grand annual cultural and religious celebration held in Cebu City to honor the Santo Niño (Child Jesus). Known for its vibrant street dancing, traditional costumes, and rhythmic drumbeats, Sinulog is one of the Philippines’ most iconic and colorful festivals. The name “Sinulog” comes from the Cebuano word sulog, meaning “like water current,” describing the forward-backward dance movement performed in honor of the Santo Niño.

HISTORY

The origins of Sinulog date back to 1521, when Ferdinand Magellan arrived in the Philippines and gifted the image of the Santo Niño to Rajah Humabon and Queen Juana after their baptism into Christianity. It is said that Queen Juana danced with joy while holding the image, a gesture that inspired the modern Sinulog dance. Over centuries, this spiritual ritual evolved into a full-blown festival. In 1980, Cebu City organized the first official Sinulog Festival, combining Catholic devotion with pre-Hispanic dance traditions, turning it into a national cultural treasure.
