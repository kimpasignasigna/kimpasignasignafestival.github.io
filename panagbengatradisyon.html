Dance

The Panagbenga Festival dance is a vibrant street dance parade featuring performers dressed in colorful, flower-inspired costumes, often inspired by the Bendian, an Ibaloi dance of celebration from the Cordilleras. These dances celebrate Baguio City's culture and the spirit of the Cordillera region, with participants moving in harmony to the beat of indigenous and contemporary music.

Cloth

Panagbenga Festival costumes are vibrant and colorful, often featuring flower-inspired designs and elements of Cordilleran native attire. Dancers wear loose-fitting blouses with intricate embroidery and flowing skirts adorned with floral patterns. Accessories like floral earrings, necklaces, and belts, as well as "bakya" or "tsinelas" (light footwear), add to the festive look.

Music

The music at the Panagbenga Festival is vibrant and lively, blending traditional and modern rhythms to create a festive atmosphere. It features a mix of indigenous sounds and contemporary tunes, often played by marching bands and drum and lyre groups, and is designed to energize participants and spectators.
