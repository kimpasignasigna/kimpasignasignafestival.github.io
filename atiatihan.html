ATI-ATIHAN Festival
DESCRIPTION

Ati-Atihan is known as the “Mother of All Philippine Festivals.” It is held in honor of the Santo Niño (Holy Child) and is famous for its tribal, war-paint-inspired face and body decorations.

HISTORY

The name comes from "Ati," the indigenous people of Panay. The festival originated from a peace pact between Malay settlers and the Ati people. Later, it was Christianized and associated with the Santo Niño.
