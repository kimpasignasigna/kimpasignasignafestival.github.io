KADAYAWAN Festival
DESCRIPTION

The Kadayawan Festival is an annual celebration in Davao City, Philippines, known for its vibrant parades, fruit and flower displays, and lively street dances. It's a thanksgiving festival for nature's bounty, cultural heritage, and bountiful harvests. Originally, it was known as the Apo Duwaling Festival, symbolizing Mt. Apo, the durian, and the Waling-Waling orchid.

HISTORY

The Kadayawan Festival in Davao City has roots in ancient thanksgiving traditions of local tribes honoring their deities for bountiful harvests. In the 1970s, then Mayor Elias B. Lopez began incorporating these traditions into city festivals. The festival, initially named "Apo Duwaling" in 1986 to unite the city's diverse population, was officially renamed "Kadayawan" in 1988 by Mayor Rodrigo Duterte. The name "Kadayawan" comes from the Mandaya word "madayaw," meaning "good" or "beautiful," reflecting the festival's celebration of abundance and unity.
