PAHIYAS Festival
DESCRIPTION

The Pahiyas Festival is a vibrant Philippine festival held annually in Lucban, Quezon, on May 15th. It's a celebration of thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest, honoring San Isidro Labrador, the patron saint of farmers. The festival is known for its colorful displays of agricultural produce and creatively decorated homes, particularly those adorned with colorful kiping (rice wafers) in elaborate shapes.

HISTORY

Pahiyas Festival comes from the word pahiyas that means thanksgiving. Its origin harks back to the time before the coming of the Spaniards. The practice of offering to St. Isidore is believed to originate from pre-Christian rite of allocating a portion of the harvest to ancestor spirits. Townsfolk engaged in merry-making by holding a feast and drinking native alcoholic beverage, believing that doing so would ensure another year of good harvest.
