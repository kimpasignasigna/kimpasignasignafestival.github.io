ATI-ATIHAN Festival
DESCRIPTION

The Ati-Atihan Festival in Kalibo, Aklan, Philippines, is a vibrant and unique celebration that honors the Sto. Ni√±o (Infant Jesus) while also acknowledging the indigenous Ati people, the original inhabitants of Panay Island. The festival's name translates to "imitating the Ati " and involves participants smearing themselves with soot and dressing in elaborate tribal costumes, mimicking the appearance of the Ati people. It's a lively event with music, dance, and processions, culminating in the "Sadsad," a unique dance where people drag their feet in time to the beat.

HISTORY

The Ati-Atihan Festival in Kalibo, Aklan, Philippines, has a rich history rooted in the island's indigenous Ati people and the arrival of Malay settlers in the 13th century. Initially, the festival celebrated the peace agreement and cooperation between the Ati and the Malays, with the Malays adopting the Ati's soot-painted faces and tribal attire as a sign of respect and unity.
